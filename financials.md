## What the community handed over — 7 May 2020

Until 2020 the farm was run by the Rylington Park Management Committee Incorporated under a lease from the Shire. The audited 2019/20 Annual Report records it plainly:

> "Rylington Park Management Committee Incorporated occupied Rylington Park as lessee and conducted the Rylington Park Agricultural Operations under the lease. The lease expired on the termination date and was not extended..."

> "On 7 May 2020 the Shire acquired 100% control and conduct of the Rylington Park Agricultural Operations from the Rylington Park Management Committee Incorporated."

On that day the following was transferred to the Shire at fair value, free from encumbrances and at no cost to the Shire:

|---|---|
| Investment account cash | 661,555 |
| Biological assets — sheep | 480,894 |
| Stock on hand — grain | 28,950 |
| Stock on hand — fuel | 2,103 |
| Buildings | 22,328 |
| Plant & equipment | 182,208 |
| **Total assets** | **1,378,038** |
| Less GST payable | (5,125) |
| **Net assets** | **1,372,913** |

The Shire recorded the $1,372,913 as an in-kind contribution.

Before the handover, the Committee had publicly asked the community "to help retain this valuable asset as a whole for Mr Farleigh's intent as a Research and Training Facility" ([Boyup Gazette, January 2020, pp.9–10](https://www.boyupbrook.org/uploads/5/8/0/2/58027465/january_2020_for_web.pdf)). The Shire called for public submissions, closing 17 January 2020. The Council resolution that authorised the takeover agreement (16 April 2020, resolution 58/20, carried 8/1) followed discussion held in "committee of the whole", which is not recorded in the minutes — and the published minutes of that decision do not table, summarise or mention the public submissions.

## The two reserves the money went into

The cash was placed into two reserves, created in 2019/20. Their stated purposes, word for word from the audited reports (identical in all six years since):

Rylington Park Working Capital Reserve — "to be used to as working capital for the running and maintenance of the Rylington Park farm." (The wording, including "to be used to as", is the Shire's own, in every year.)

Rylington Park Community Projects Reserve — "to be used for the community contribution only towards major community projects."

## The $50,000 from the reserve, next to the law

On 15 April 2026, Council resolved (CM 26/04/084) to redirect $50,000 from the Working Capital Reserve for repairs to the homestead, in connection with approving the CEO's occupation of it — [the full decision and meeting record are here](foi-meeting.html). The resolution cites s.6.8(1)(b) of the Local Government Act 1995, the provision for expenditure "authorised in advance by resolution". It does not cite s.6.11, the provision that governs reserve accounts. What s.6.11 says, verbatim:

> "(2) Subject to subsection (3), before a local government — (a) changes the purpose of a reserve account; or (b) uses the money in a reserve account for another purpose, it must give one month's local public notice of the proposed change of purpose or proposed use."

> "(3) A local government is not required to give local public notice under subsection (2) — (a) where the change of purpose or of proposed use of money has been disclosed in the annual budget of the local government for that financial year; or (b) in such other circumstances as are prescribed."

Placed next to that text, the published record shows:

The reserve's stated purpose is working capital for "the running and maintenance of the Rylington Park farm". The $50,000 was redirected to homestead repairs in connection with the CEO's occupation.

The officer report for the 15 April item itself states there was "currently no specific allocation in the adopted 2025/2026 Annual Budget for these works" — the words that would otherwise engage the budget-disclosure exception in s.6.11(3)(a). The adopted 2025/26 budget planned zero transfers from this reserve.

The prescribed exceptions (Local Government (Financial Management) Regulations 1996, reg 18) cover presidential emergency authorisations, amounts up to $5,000 a year, and states of emergency. The 15 April transfer was authorised by ordinary resolution, was $50,000, and no state of emergency declaration was in force.

No record of one month's local public notice preceding the 15 April 2026 decision has been located in the published record. Readers can compare the legislation and the record for themselves.

Sources: [Local Government Act 1995 (WA), s.6.11](https://classic.austlii.edu.au/au/legis/wa/consol_act/lga1995182/s6.11.html) and [s.6.8](https://classic.austlii.edu.au/au/legis/wa/consol_act/lga1995182/s6.8.html); [Local Government (Financial Management) Regulations 1996, reg 18](https://classic.austlii.edu.au/au/legis/wa/consol_reg/lgmr1996434/s18.html); Special Council Minutes 15 April 2026 ([Shire document 1477](https://www.boyupbrook.wa.gov.au/documents/1477/special-council-minutes-15-april-2026)); adopted 2025/26 budget (special council attachment 7 August 2025, p.20).

## The livestock, and the quarantine promise

On 26 February 2026, when Council resolved to move to the leasing model (CM 26/02/030), it also resolved to sell all livestock "as soon as practical, but before 30 January 2027", and directed, verbatim:

> "Proceeds of the sale of the machinery, produce and livestock be quarantined in a reserve account solely for the use to reinstate Rylington Park should the Council of the day wish to no longer lease the farm and to run the farm."

The Shire President repeated that commitment publicly in a media release of 25 March 2026: "Council also resolved that proceeds from the sale of machinery, produce and livestock be quarantined in a reserve account to allow for the possible reinstatement of farming operations in the future."

What the published record then shows:

Stock program income (ledger code GL149105) reached **$605,675** by 31 March 2026 and stayed at that figure through 30 April and 31 May — against an amended budget of $368,189.

The "Rylington Park Re-establishment Reserve" — the quarantine reserve the resolution called for — was created on 25 June 2026 (CM 26/06/137, carried 7/0) and seeded with **$6,406.03**: the net proceeds of one sale of 47 rams at Katanning on 3 June 2026 ($6,900 gross less $493.97 costs), described in the report as "the first stage of the de-stocking program". The same report states that future proceeds "may also be transferred to the reserve in accordance with future Council decisions" — not automatically.

Nothing in the published record shows the remaining **$599,269** of stock program income being placed in any reserve. Ledger income is pooled operating money unless Council resolves to move it to a reserve; on the face of the published record, only the June ram sale has been moved.

The de-stocking shows in the audited head counts too: 3,308 sheep were transferred to the Shire on 7 May 2020; at 30 June 2025 the count was 1,887. The 2024/25 reconciliation records 1,829 head sold and 1,177 head under "Deaths, Slaughtered and Variance" — the corresponding line the previous year was 357.

## Approved withdrawals that never posted

Two Council-approved withdrawals from the Working Capital Reserve are on the record for 2025/26: $35,000 for tractor repairs (CM 26/02/024, 26 February 2026 — traceable to matching equipment payments totalling $35,605.44) and the $50,000 for the homestead (CM 26/04/084, 15 April 2026).

The officer report of 15 April projected the reserve would hold "approximately $178,809" after the $50,000. That projection reconciles with the reserve's then-current balance once both approved withdrawals are counted ($263,810 at 31 March, less $35,000, less $50,000 = $178,810).

What does not reconcile is the reserve's own ledger: across five consecutive monthly reports — 28 February, 26 March, 30 April, 28 May and 25 June 2026 — the Working Capital Reserve's "Transfer (From)" column records $0 every month. Neither the $35,000 nor the $50,000 has been posted as a withdrawal from the reserve, while the tractor and homestead payments themselves appear, paid, in the Shire's payment lists.

One further records anomaly: council minutes of 26 March 2026 (CM 26/03/072) label GL account 1300102000 — the Working Capital Reserve's account number — as "IT Reserve" for a $17,000 transfer, one month after the February minutes used the same account number for the tractor transfer under its correct name. The annual report lists the IT/Office Equipment Reserve and the Rylington Park Working Capital Reserve as separate reserves.

Sources: Council minutes 26 February 2026 p.50, 26 March 2026 p.117; Special Council Minutes 15 April 2026 ([Shire document 1477](https://www.boyupbrook.wa.gov.au/documents/1477/special-council-minutes-15-april-2026)); monthly financial reports February–May 2026 in the council attachments of 26 March, 30 April, 28 May and 25 June 2026.
