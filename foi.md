## The story so far

**15 April 2026** — Special Council Meeting (5:30–5:44pm). Late Item 7.1.1: Council approves $50,000 from the Rylington Park Working Capital Reserve for homestead renovation and approves CEO occupation of the homestead (Decision CM 26/04/084, carried 6/0). [Details and sources →](foi-meeting.html)

**19 June 2026** — Freedom of Information application lodged with the Shire under the Freedom of Information Act 1992 (WA), seeking pre-meeting communications, any occupancy agreement, the confidential committee papers behind the decision, and the accounting and procurement records for the renovation. [The full application (PDF)](documents/FOI_Rylington_Park_19_June_2026.pdf)

**25 June 2026** — The Shire replies with an estimated charge of $240.00 — "$30.00 per hour with a minimum processing time of 8 hours". No such minimum exists in WA law. The email was signed only "Shire", with no officer named.

**5 July 2026** — Written challenge to the charge sent to the Shire: notice of intention to proceed under s.19(1)(b), with a corrected, itemised estimate — reduced 25% as the law requires for pension concession card holders — requested within 14 days.

**8 July 2026** — With no corrected estimate received, an application for internal review of the charge is lodged under s.40 of the Act (the right to review an unreasonable charge is s.39(2)(a)(vi); no fee is payable for the review, s.44).

**Coming up** — Under s.43(2) the Shire has 15 days to decide the internal review (to about 23 July 2026); if it does not, the decision is treated as confirmed, which opens the way to external review by the Information Commissioner. Separately, the Shire's 45-day period under the Act to deal with the application itself expires on 3 August 2026.

## The $240 charge, next to the law

**What the Shire said (25 June 2026):** "$30.00 per hour with a minimum processing time of 8 hours, totalling an estimated charge of $240.00." No breakdown of the 8 hours was given. The email was signed only "Shire", with no officer named.

**What the law says:** Freedom of Information Regulations 1993 (WA), Schedule 1, item 2(a): staff time is charged at $30.00 "per hour, or pro rata for a part of an hour." No minimum processing time appears anywhere in the FOI Act 1992 or the FOI Regulations 1993. "Pro rata for a part of an hour" is the opposite of a minimum: agencies must charge for actual time only.

What the FOI Act requires of an agency charging for time:

- **s.16(1)(a)** — a time-based charge "must only cover the time that would be spent by the agency in conducting a routine search for the document". The Shire's email said its estimate covers time to "search for, retrieve, assess, and prepare documents for release".
- **s.16(1)(h)** — a charge "must not exceed such amount as may be prescribed by regulation". An 8-hour minimum is not prescribed by any regulation.
- **s.17(3)** — if the estimate exceeds $25, the agency must notify the applicant of the estimate and "the basis on which its estimate is made". No basis or breakdown was provided.
- **reg 3(b)** — for the holder of a current Commonwealth pensioner concession card, any charge "is reduced by 25%". The applicant's concession card is held on the Shire's records; the estimate made no allowance for it. Even on the Shire's own 8-hour figure, the lawful maximum would be $180, not $240.
- **s.16(2)** — payment of a charge is not required before the agency has notified its decision to grant access.
- **s.39(2)(a)(vi) and s.44** — an applicant may seek internal review of a charge they consider unreasonable, and no fee is payable for that review.

## The challenge — 5 July 2026

On 5 July 2026 a written response was sent to the Shire's FOI Officer. In summary, it:

- confirms the intention to proceed with the application (s.19(1)(b)), so the 45-day statutory period keeps running;
- asks the Shire to identify the statutory source of the claimed 8-hour minimum, or withdraw it;
- asks that any component of the estimate not authorised by s.16(1) and Schedule 1 be removed;
- asks for the basis of the estimate required by s.17(3): the hours attributed to each Part of the application, the tasks comprising those hours, and the name and designation of the person who calculated it;
- claims the mandatory 25% pensioner concession reduction under reg 3(b);
- asks that the application and all charge decisions be dealt with by an officer other than the CEO, since the application concerns the CEO's own occupation of the homestead — noting that under s.39(3)(a) a decision made by the principal officer is not subject to internal review;
- requests a corrected, itemised estimate within 14 days.

## The internal review — 8 July 2026

No corrected estimate having been received, an application for internal review of the charge was lodged on 8 July 2026 under s.40 of the Act. In summary, it puts four grounds:

- there is no lawful "minimum processing time" — Schedule 1 item 2(a) charges actual time "per hour, or pro rata for a part of an hour";
- chargeable time is limited by s.16(1)(a) to a routine search — time spent assessing documents or preparing them for release is not chargeable under that provision;
- no basis for the estimate was given, as s.17(2) and (3) require — a flat figure of 8 hours, with no breakdown, is not a basis;
- the mandatory 25% pensioner concession reduction (reg 3(b)) was ignored, despite the Shire holding the card details.

It also notes that the charge decision was unsigned and the decision-maker not identified.
