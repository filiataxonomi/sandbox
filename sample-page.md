This page shows every kind of content the real site uses, so you can see the plain-text version and the finished result side by side.

## A section heading (two hashes)

An ordinary paragraph. Just type. Leave one blank line between paragraphs. That is the whole trick.

### A smaller heading (three hashes)

Text can be **bold** with two asterisks, or *italic* with one. A link to a Shire document looks like this in the file: `[Council Minutes 30 April 2026](https://www.boyupbrook.wa.gov.au)` and comes out like this: [Council Minutes 30 April 2026](https://www.boyupbrook.wa.gov.au).

> "Without creating any specific trust it is my wish that my farming property be retained by the Shire of Boyup Brook for the benefit of persons within the Shire and for research purposes."

Source: Will of Eric Farleigh, 8 June 1988, as quoted in the Charter.
{: .src}

## A list

A list is lines starting with a hyphen and a space:

- Demonstrate good governance and leadership.
- Deliver industry relevant training.
- Provide regular updates to the Boyup Brook community.

## A numbered list

1. First point.
2. Second point.
3. Third point.

## Linking to a PDF in the documents folder

If a PDF called `FOI_Rylington_Park_19_June_2026.pdf` sits in the site's `documents` folder, you link to it like this: [Read the FOI application (PDF)](documents/FOI_Rylington_Park_19_June_2026.pdf).

## An image

An image is a link with an exclamation mark in front: `![description of image](payid-brand.png)` — the description shows if the image fails to load.
