## Text appearing in the published agenda and minutes

This item is placed at the top of this page deliberately, to bring it to the community's attention. Both the published agenda and the published minutes for Item 7.1.1 — the report recommending $50,000 of reserve funds and CEO occupation of the homestead — contain the following text, word for word:

> **"If you would like, I can also turn this into a more polished final agenda-ready version with a nominated GL funding source and a stronger argument linked to future leasing/use of the homestead."**

That is an offer to produce "a more polished final agenda-ready version" with "a stronger argument" — and it sits in both official published documents of the Shire of Boyup Brook. It is reproduced here exactly as published, so readers can see the tone for themselves and judge what it indicates about how the item was prepared. No further comment is made.

## What the meeting decided

The meeting dealt with one substantive matter, brought as late Item 7.1.1: "Proposed Budget Amendment to Facilitate Repairs on Rylington Park Homestead". The decision, verbatim from the published minutes:

> **Council Decision CM 26/04/084** — That Council Resolves to:
>
> 1. Pursuant to section 6.8(1)(b) of the Local Government Act 1995, amends the 2025/2026 budget to redirect $50,000 from the Rylington Park Working Capital Reserve to Rylington Operational Expenses - Materials for repairs required to the Rylington Park Homestead.
> 2. Requests the Chief Executive Officer to include a further $50,000 for Rylington Park Homestead repairs for Council's consideration in the draft 2026/2027 Annual Budget.
> 3. Approves the retention of the workshop as part of the homestead area for the use of the occupant of the Rylington Park Homestead.
> 4. Approves the occupation of the Rylington Park Homestead by the Chief Executive Officer.
> 5. Notes that occupation of the Rylington Park Homestead will enable the Rogers Avenue property to be made available for rental through GROW Housing, subject to the completion of the necessary arrangements.
>
> CARRIED BY ABSOLUTE MAJORITY 6/0

## What the published record shows about the item

The report for Item 7.1.1 was authored by the Chief Executive Officer, Leonard Long — the same person whose occupation of the homestead the report recommends. The item header records the author's Declaration of Interest as "Nil".

The item header also records: Previous Items — Nil. Attachments — Nil. There were no quotes, no scope of works, no valuation, no lease and no rent figure anywhere in the published papers. Under Consultation the report records consultation as having occurred "internally" only.

The meeting ran from 5:30pm to 5:44pm. Members of the public left the chamber at 5:36pm; there was no public question time. Two of the six votes were cast by councillors attending their first council meeting, sworn in that same evening.
