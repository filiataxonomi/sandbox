Every entry on this page comes from the Shire of Boyup Brook's published council and committee minutes, attachments and media release. Each entry states its source. Where the record is silent, this page says so rather than guessing.

## The direction

On 26 February 2026, as part of Council Decision CM 26/02/030 (the change to a leasing-based operational model for Rylington Park), Council resolved, at point 10:

> "Proceeds of the sale of the machinery, produce and livestock be quarantined in a reserve account solely for the use to reinstate Rylington Park should the Council of the day wish to no longer lease the farm and to run the farm."

Source: Ordinary Council Meeting Minutes, 26 February 2026, item 10.4.8, Council Decision CM 26/02/030, p.81 — [Council Minutes 26 February 2026](https://www.boyupbrook.wa.gov.au/documents/1464/council-minutes-26-february-2026).
{: .src}

## The direction appears three times in the record

**26 February 2026 — the Council decision.** Point 10 of CM 26/02/030, quoted above. Moved Cr Inglis, seconded Cr Wright, carried by absolute majority 5/0. The same decision set the sale timetable: all livestock to be sold "as soon as practical, but before 30 January 2027" (point 1(e)); an "On Farm" auction of all farm equipment in January/February 2027 (point 1(d)); and the farm lease to commence 1 March 2027 (point 1(c)(ii)).

Source: Ordinary Council Meeting Minutes, 26 February 2026, pp.79–81.
{: .src}

**25 March 2026 — the Shire President's public statement.** The Shire published a media release, "Council Endorses Revised Operational Model for Rylington Park," signed by Cr Helen O'Connell, Shire President:

> "Council also resolved that proceeds from the sale of machinery, produce and livestock be quarantined in a reserve account to allow for the possible reinstatement of farming operations in the future."

Source: [Media Release, 25 March 2026, on the Shire website](https://www.boyupbrook.wa.gov.au/news/rylington-park-model/729) — the release text is in the file embedded on that page.
{: .src}

**26 March 2026 — the Rylington Park Committee's own papers.** The officer report before the Rylington Park Committee (item 6.1.4, "Decommissioning of Shire Operated Farming Activities"; Author and Title: "Leonard Long, Chief Executive Officer") recommended, at point 4:

> "Approves that any unexpended operating funds and net sale proceeds arising from the cessation of the Shire operated farming activities at Rylington Park be transferred to the Rylington Park Reinstatement Farm Reserve."

The report's Resource Implications section stated: "The immediate sale of livestock and surplus farming assets may also generate proceeds that can be directed to the Rylington Park Reserve and used to support future Rylington Park purposes."

Source: Rylington Park Committee Minutes, 26 March 2026, item 6.1.4, pp.24 and 32 — [Committee Minutes 26 March 2026](https://www.boyupbrook.wa.gov.au/Profiles/boyupbrook/Assets/ClientData/2026-03-26_Rylington_Park_Minutes.pdf).
{: .src}

The name "Rylington Park Reinstatement Farm Reserve" appears both in Council's own February decision (CM 26/02/030, point 13) and in that deferred committee recommendation. The reserve actually established on 25 June 2026 (CM 26/06/137) is named the "Rylington Park Re-establishment Reserve." The published record does not state whether these are the same reserve under a different name.

No standalone Monthly Financial Report closing at 31 December 2025 appears in the published record; the 31 January 2026 year-to-date figure covers December.

The 26 March deferral is where the published decision trail ends. The Rylington Park Committee deferred the CEO's immediate-sale recommendation to the farm visit and its next meeting; no outcome of the visit was published, no later Rylington Park Committee minutes have been published, and when Council received the 26 March minutes on 30 April (CM 26/04/111) it made no separate decision on the deferred recommendation. Meanwhile the ledger figure rose by $210,113 in the period ending 31 March 2026 — five days after the deferral — and the only two payments in the year's records described as "Sheep Freight" to D&L Bleechmore Haulage are dated 30 March and 7 April, either side of that period's end. The monthly reports give cumulative totals only, so what was sold, when and to whom does not appear in any published document.

The $6,406.03 transfer is the only movement of stock sale proceeds into a reserve account anywhere in the published record. The remaining income stayed in the Shire's municipal fund as ordinary operating revenue; no other transfer to a reserve appears in the published record. The 25 June 2026 report states that future net proceeds "may also be transferred to the reserve in accordance with future Council decisions."

Money was moving in the other direction: the two Council-approved withdrawals FROM the Rylington Park Working Capital Reserve in the same period — $35,000 for tractor repairs (CM 26/02/024, 26 February) and $50,000 for the homestead (CM 26/04/084, 15 April) — had, as at the 31 May 2026 monthly report, still not been posted in the reserve's own "Transfer (From)" column, while the tractor and homestead payments appear, paid, in the Shire's payment lists. The reserve movements year by year are on [the Financials page](financials.html).

## After 31 March 2026

The Presidential Communications and Reports by Elected Members sections of the Ordinary Council Meeting minutes for 30 April, 28 May and 25 June 2026 contain no mention of the 31 March 2026 farm visit or of any livestock sale. The single Rylington Park mention in those sections is Cr Wright's 25 June 2026 report on the ECU Sheep Industry Pivot Project, an unrelated grant matter.

No Rylington Park Committee minutes dated after 26 March 2026 have been published (checked against the Shire's committee minutes index, 14 July 2026).
